FocusKiller Privacy Policy

Effective Date: [Insert Date]

1. Data Collection

FocusKiller (“we”, “our”, or “the Extension”) does not collect personal information or track your browsing activity. The extension only stores the following data locally or in your Chrome Sync storage to provide functionality:

  • Blocked Websites List: The domains you choose to block.
  • Focus Timer Settings: Duration of focus sessions and break times.
  • Focus Session Statistics: Daily focus time and streaks.

This data is used solely to enable FocusKiller’s core functionality — helping you stay focused and productive.

2. Data Usage

The data stored is used only locally on your device or synced across your devices via Chrome Sync.

  • We do not share, sell, or transmit your data to any external servers.
  • Data is used only to remember your settings and track your focus sessions.

3. Permissions

FocusKiller requires the following permissions to operate correctly:

  • declarativeNetRequest & declarativeNetRequestWithHostAccess – To block or redirect distracting websites.
  • Host Permissions – To apply blocking rules only to the websites you choose.
  • Storage – To save your preferences and statistics.

These permissions are essential for the extension’s single purpose: blocking distracting websites during focus sessions.

4. Remote Code

FocusKiller does not load or execute any remote code. All code is packaged within the extension.

5. User Control

You can:

  • Add or remove blocked websites at any time.
  • Reset focus session statistics.
  • Disable or remove the extension whenever you want.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy occasionally. Updates will be reflected in the published version of the policy.
